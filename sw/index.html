Introductions

Hi!

My name is Jack Conger.

When I was still a baby in the cold north of Alaska, my dad, a certified Windows know-it-all, instilled in me an addiction to computers and problem-solving.

I've since switched to Linux and gained a passion for free and open-source software, as well as a driving urge for finding elegant solutions at the root of problems I find—hence, I am pursuing a math and computer science double major at the University of Washington in Seattle.

Believe in the Non-Sexy

I believe in non-sexy software: software people don't even notice, even though they use it every day of their lives; software that can be truly helpful to those in need, not simply an incremental improvement for affluent, privileged people in affluent, privileged parts of the world; software that is old, ugly, and infrastructural.

Can you imagine if Tim Berners-Lee had made his WorldWideWeb the way Mark Zuckerberg made Facebook?
