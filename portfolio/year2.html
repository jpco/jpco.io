College, Year 2

Honors 232

Honors 232, “Piracy”, represented my first and only academic encounter with the infamous Clarke Speed. Before I entered the class, I knew many people who had taken classes with him before, and their opinion of him was very polarized—which, knowing him, is the norm with his past students. My curiosity, and the rumours he'd been fired, drove me to the class.

The course itself was good. Primarily, I think it was worthwhile as an exercise in a non-typical kind of discourse, driven mostly by key terms and very exploratory. Some of the material itself, and especially the in-class discussions, was frustrating. One discussion in particular was somewhat about feminism and was very frustrating, which led to my artifact for this class: my final paper (.pdf, source here).

Math 336

Math 336 marked the end of what I am convinced was the hardest series I will take at the UW. Reaching the early graduate level, math 33X covers six 300- and 400-level mathematics classes, and leaves only 27 credits required to finish the comprehensive mathematics major.

One major aspect of the math 13X and 33X series is the process of beginning to think like a postgraduate mathematician. And, of course, part of this is research. Because of this, math 336 has as a final component a research paper dedicated to the short explanation of some journal article and the mathematical framework required to understand that article. I chose to write about combinatorics on words, a relatively new field in math dedicated to studying the types of sequences created by certain sets of symbols. My research is summarized in my paper (source).
